#Main Themes#


 AI SEO Principles:


Search has decentralized; LLMs and AI agents are now primary discovery channels

Traditional click-through metrics no longer measure success; prompt tracking and visibility matter more

Entity relationships and knowledge graph clarity supersede keyword targeting

Content must demonstrate genuine expertise to survive in reasoning-model results



 Content Strategy Lessons:


Avoid "checklist SEO"; creativity, experimentation, and curiosity drive competitive advantage

Multimodal content (video, text, structured data, owned data) increases visibility across AI and traditional search

Earned media (journalist citations, news coverage) is a leverage point in LLM-generated answers

Build unique, proprietary datasets; when brands own their data, AI attribution becomes unavoidable



 B2B SaaS Takeaways:


Focus on conversational queries; B2B buyers ask complex, multi-layered questions that reasoning models prioritize

Optimize for machine-readability and API compatibility; AI agents will skip companies without real-time data integration

Two distinct jobs now exist: human-focused SEO (clicks, engagement) and AI-focused optimization (data supply, entity clarity)

Smaller players can compete by specializing in niches where they own unique data or relationships



 Actionable Insights :



Immediate: Audit brand entity presence across Wikipedia, Wikidata, schema markup, and knowledge graphs

Short-term: Monitor LLM crawling behavior (OpenAI, Meta, others crawl differently than Google)

Medium-term: Build prompt-tracking systems to measure visibility in ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity without relying on clicks

Long-term: Develop agentic-ready infrastructure (real-time inventory, pricing, API access) for AI agent commerce

Strategic: Invest in earned media and journalist relationships; news citations are anchor points for LLM-generated content
