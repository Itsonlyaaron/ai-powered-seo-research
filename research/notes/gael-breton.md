AI SEO Principles:

GEO = SEO evolved: 95% of LLM visibility comes from the same information retrieval and entity signals that drive organic rankings.

Entity > keyword: Brand strength, topical authority, and entity relationships are the primary levers in an AI-driven search environment.

Claude Code for automated SEO: AI agents paired with MCP servers can now execute SEO tasks (crawl, analyse, update) autonomously — not just assist humans.

Content quality threshold has risen: Mass-produced AI content triggers deindexing; tight, expert-led content clusters with genuine depth perform in both Google and LLMs.

LLM brand monitoring is non-negotiable: Brands must actively track and correct how they are represented inside LLMs (off-page GEO).


Content Strategy Lessons :

AI should function as a "creative calculator" — producing scale and variants — while humans control direction and final editorial voice.

Feedback and refinement loops are what separate high-quality AI content from detectable, low-value output.

Content still matters in the era of AI agents; the form is changing (interactive tools, structured knowledge bases, community-led content), but the need to be cited by authoritative sources has not changed.

Updating stale content (e.g., "Best X in 2022" titles in 2025/2026) is one of the highest-leverage, lowest-effort SEO tasks most sites ignore.


B2B SaaS Takeaways :
AI sales-enablement tools that cannot be trivially replicated in ChatGPT or Gemini create genuine competitive moats.

Automated cold outreach systems (Breton reports 2–3 qualified leads per day from an automated system) replace low-leverage BDR time.

AI-driven member retention systems (cancellation-risk databases, personalised save offers) are high-ROI internal automations for SaaS operators.

Community and brand signals increasingly influence both organic search rankings and LLM citation — B2B SaaS companies underinvesting in brand are exposed.


Actionable Insights:

Audit your content for stale date signals: Any title or meta referencing a year older than 12 months is bleeding CTR. Update and re-date.

Use Gemini Deep Research for B2B opportunity mapping: Ask it to surface industry questions your competitors' content does not fully answer.

Install MCP servers to automate SEO execution: Pair Claude Code with an Ahrefs MCP or similar to have an agent identify, prioritise, and implement on-site SEO changes.

Track GA4 for AI-driven sessions: Filter engagement by channel to identify which pages AI search is already surfacing — then build more content in those clusters.

Build entity-rich author profiles and sameAs schema: Real author credentials and entity markup are the post-HCU minimum for any site targeting competitive keywords.

Treat internal links as non-negotiable: Every published page without 2–3 internal links is structurally isolated and sending a negative signal to Google's graph.

Test GEO before investing heavily: Verify whether AI chatbot traffic converts in your niche before redirecting significant resources from proven channels.
