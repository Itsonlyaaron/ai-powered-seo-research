Who Is Matt Diggity?

Matt Diggity is a former electrical engineer turned SEO entrepreneur and angel investor based in Chiang Mai, Thailand. He is the founder of Diggity Marketing, LeadSpring, The Search Initiative (an international SEO agency), Diggity Media (PPC), and Epic Video, and was formerly co-founder of Authority Builders and an investor/advisor to Surfer SEO — both of which he has since exited. He organizes the Chiang Mai SEO Conference, one of the most respected practitioner-focused SEO events globally. His content brand is built on a single promise: test-based SEO with real data, no theory.


Main Themes:

-Matt's 2024–2026 content centers on three converging forces: the collapse of traditional SEO as the sole traffic lever, the rise of AI as both a search channel and a content production tool, and the growing importance of brand equity as the foundational ranking signal across all surfaces — Google, AI Overviews, and LLM-powered search engines.


AI SEO Principles:


-AI search engines (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews) do not rank pages the way Google does — they cite entities and brands based on editorial trust signals distributed across the open web.

-Editorial media (press, third-party mentions, brand references) now constitutes 61% of what AI systems surface — owning your content is necessary but not sufficient.

-Google cannot reliably detect AI-generated content at scale, but mass AI content production triggers velocity-based manual reviews — content quality and publishing cadence both matter.

-GEO (Generative Engine Optimization) is the emerging discipline for AI search visibility, requiring a fundamentally different strategy than traditional keyword-based SEO.

-Brand search volume — the frequency with which users query your brand name directly — is now a measurable, engineerable ranking signal, not a passive byproduct of visibility.



Content Strategy Lessons:


-AI tools cut content production costs by orders of magnitude, but human oversight remains essential for NLP-friendly structure, search intent alignment, and quality control.

-YouTube is Matt's highest-leverage content channel: it builds brand search volume, generates organic reach, and has a negative effective customer acquisition cost.

-Traffic diversity (social, direct, paid, referral, YouTube) is both a ranking signal and a business resilience strategy — single-channel dependence on Google is a structural risk.

-Topical authority still matters, but 200+ pages is the new baseline for competitive niches; 20-page niche sites no longer hold up.

-Monthly SEO news roundups are a high-retention content format that Matt has used consistently to maintain audience engagement without requiring original research in every issue.



B2B SaaS Takeaway :


-Technical SEO is dramatically underutilized by funded startups — developer-built sites routinely fail basic indexability standards, wasting significant paid media budget.
Fixing title tags, internal linking architecture, and buyer-keyword targeting can 5x organic traffic within 30 days for sites with existing page equity.

-For SaaS and B2B companies, AI search visibility is not a future concern — it is a present blind spot, since 61% of AI-generated responses are drawn from editorial sources that most SaaS companies are absent from.

-Diversifying beyond Google (Reddit, LinkedIn, YouTube, programmatic PR) is how SaaS companies can build AI search citations proactively rather than reactively.

-Angel investing in SEO-adjacent startups is Matt's described "final form" of SEO monetization — operators with proven organic growth skills can generate asymmetric returns as advisors and investors.



Actionable Insights:


-Audit your AI visibility now. Search your brand in ChatGPT and Perplexity. If you're not cited, you're invisible to a growing share of buyer intent.

-Invest in editorial media immediately. Build a PR strategy targeting digital publications where your category is actively covered — this is the top driver of AI citations.

-Engineer brand search volume deliberately. Get featured in "best of" roundups, run brand awareness ads, publish on YouTube, and pursue Reddit and LinkedIn mentions in your niche.

-Fix technical SEO before scaling content. Indexed pages without proper title tags, internal links, and buyer keyword targeting produce near-zero organic returns.

-Diversify traffic sources as a ranking strategy. Social traffic and branded direct traffic are positive ranking signals in their own right, not just vanity metrics.

-Integrate AI into content production at the strategy layer, not just execution. Use AI for topical mapping and content briefs; apply human review for NLP structure and intent alignment.

-Hire only A-players. In a knowledge business, one underperformer degrades the performance of the entire team — the cultural cost exceeds the salary savings.
