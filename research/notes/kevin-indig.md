Who Kevin Indig Is
Kevin Indig (born October 3, 1987) is one of the most cited practitioners in enterprise and growth SEO. He built his reputation leading SEO and growth functions at three category-defining B2B companies — Atlassian, G2, and Shopify — before transitioning to independent advisory work in mid-2022. He has helped companies acquire over 100 million users through organic search and has built and led in-house SEO teams of 25 or more people.


Current Role / Company
Independent Growth Advisor — operating under his personal brand at kevin-indig.com and as a Partner at HyperGrowth Partners, a sweat-equity advisory firm for Series A–C B2B SaaS companies. He advises a portfolio that includes Meta, Reddit, Ramp, Snapchat, Dropbox, Bounce, Toast, and Nextdoor, among others. He is also an angel investor in more than 12 startups.
He publishes Growth Memo (growth-memo.com), a research-backed newsletter reaching more than 22,000 subscribers, and co-hosts the Contrarian Marketing podcast with Eli Schwartz. He also writes regularly for Search Engine Journal.


Why He Is Relevant to AI-Powered SEO
Indig sits at the precise intersection of the topics this research project covers:
He runs original quantitative research on AI search behaviour (250-session AI Mode study; 1.2M ChatGPT citation analysis), not just opinion.
He advises B2B SaaS companies at growth stage — the exact profile of the target audience for this repository.
He has been consistently early on the structural implications of AI for organic growth: the decoupling of traffic from revenue, the rise of LLM citation as a new SEO signal, and the death of keyword-volume as the primary content investment criterion.
His frameworks (topic-first SEO, the Great Decoupling, brand-SEO strategy) are immediately actionable and reference real client data.
