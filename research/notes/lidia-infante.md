Who Is Lidia Infante?

Lidia Infante is a Spanish-born SEO strategist, speaker, and writer based in the UK, now operating globally. She entered digital marketing through an unconventional route — founding a feminist magazine in Spain that grew to 750K monthly pageviews, which gave her firsthand experience in building organic reach from scratch. She holds a BSc in Psychology and an MSc in Digital Business, and she has leveraged her psychology background throughout her career to inform audience-first content strategy and stakeholder communication.

She is recognised as one of the most prominent voices in the SEO industry internationally, speaking regularly at MozCon, BrightonSEO, SMX, WTSFest, and international summits. She is also a passionate advocate for women in tech and has produced annual data reports on the gender gap in SEO publishing.

What Does She Do?

Lidia leads SEO at the enterprise SaaS level, combining technical SEO, content strategy, international expansion, and data analysis. She is also a prolific writer for publications including Search Engine Journal, Moz, and the Wix SEO Hub, and she conducts original research into the SEO industry itself — covering topics like learning habits, gender representation, and content economics.

AI SEO Principles

-AI has changed the economics of content, not the fundamentals of SEO — quality and expertise remain the core differentiators.

-Using AI to understand your audience (e.g., AI-facilitated interviews, sentiment extraction) is more valuable than using it to replace your authors.

-Thin AI content is not just ineffective — it can actively degrade a site's overall search performance by adding noise that interferes with LLM retrieval.

-The combination of human expertise + AI tools + structured data (schema) is the content formula built for the current search landscape.

-SEO must now account for AI Overviews, GEO (Generative Engine Optimisation), and multi-modal search — not just the 10 blue links.


Content Strategy Lessons

-Stop writing for search volume; start writing for audience need — many high-value queries are new every day.

-Reduce content volume and raise research depth — 5 well-researched pieces outperform 10 thin ones in the AI era.

-Author identity and real-world experience are now competitive advantages, not optional extras.

-Newsletters have overtaken social media and events as the most trusted and effective learning channel — a signal for content distribution strategy.

-Content at scale must not sacrifice editorial oversight — Google's manual actions for "scaled content abuse" (June and August 2025) confirm enforcement is real.



B2B SaaS Takeaways

-At SurveyMonkey, Lidia frames SEO as a revenue growth channel, not a traffic metric — a mental model shift that is essential for getting executive buy-in in SaaS.

-International SEO at SaaS scale requires treating localisation, tech infrastructure, and product teams as partners, not dependencies.

-Headless CMS architecture is increasingly the standard for enterprise SaaS SEO — it enables content velocity, omnichannel reach, and structured data at scale.

-The cross-functional model (SEO embedded with product, marketing, and eng) is the operational pattern that consistently produces results in high-growth SaaS.

-Brand and SEO are converging — Lidia's MozCon and SMX London 2025 talk title "SEO and Brand Sitting in a Tree K-I-S-S-I-N-G" signals that she sees brand authority as an increasingly important organic ranking signal.


Actionable Insights

-Audit your internationalisation stack, not just your hreflang setup — technology gaps kill international SEO before content ever has a chance.

-Use AI to interview your audience at scale and extract genuine language and pain points for content briefs.

-Prioritise newsletter distribution for SEO content — practitioners trust newsletters over events and social media in 2025.

-Run an SEO gap analysis using competitor data to identify uncontested opportunities — a methodology Lidia has taught at MozCon and WordCamp Europe.

-Combine author expertise + AI tooling + schema markup for content that is both differentiated and machine-readable.

-Treat SEO as a cross-functional programme, not a team task — assign clear stakeholder owners across product, eng, content, and brand.

-Investigate singular vs. plural keyword intent before defaulting to the higher-volume variant — GSC impression data is your fastest signal.
