Who is Ross Simmonds?

Ross Simmonds is the Founder and CEO of Foundation Marketing, a B2B content marketing agency serving brands like Canva, Bitly, and Shopify, and the creator of Distribution.ai, a tool for scaling content repurposing. He is widely credited with popularising Generative Engine Optimisation (GEO) as a practical marketing discipline and is the author of Create Once, Distribute Forever. His research — including an 8,566-keyword study on Reddit's B2B dominance — has made him one of the most cited voices on AI search visibility, content distribution, and B2B SaaS growth strategy.


Main Themes


Distribution over creation: Publishing is the starting line, not the finish. The brands winning in 2026 treat every asset as a system to be distributed across Reddit, YouTube, LinkedIn, email, and niche communities.
GEO as mandatory infrastructure: AI visibility requires a multi-surface content presence — LLMs are trained on what is widely distributed, not what ranks on a single domain.
Reddit as a B2B growth engine: Reddit outranks most B2B vendors on nearly 1 million monthly searches and powers the majority of bottom-funnel LLM citations. It is no longer a niche channel.
The shift from traffic to influence: Success metrics have moved from clicks to citations — 70% of brand mentions in AI conversations are "dark" and invisible to standard analytics.
Media ownership as competitive moat: B2B brands that own distribution channels (newsletters, podcasts, YouTube audiences) compound advantage in ways that ad spend cannot replicate.



AI & Content Marketing Insights


The Citation Gap is the defining challenge of AI search: ChatGPT resolves queries without linking out (~12% of answers contain links), while Perplexity links in ~95% of answers. Brands must optimise differently for each — concise summaries for ChatGPT, structured and stat-dense content for Perplexity.
Original research is the fastest route to LLM citations: AI cites sources only when data is too specific to paraphrase. Proprietary studies, unique datasets, and original case studies are the clearest path to persistent AI brand mentions.
GEO rewards entity strength and multi-platform authority: AI platforms evaluate an entire content ecosystem, not just a website. Off-domain assets — Reddit threads, YouTube videos, Medium posts, G2 reviews — are now core SEO infrastructure.




Practical Takeaways for B2B SaaS


Install the Reddit pixel on pricing and comparison pages immediately — retarget Reddit visitors at 40–60% lower CPL than LinkedIn before investing in organic Reddit presence.
Audit existing content for the distribution gap — identify your top 10 performing pieces and systematically redistribute them across Reddit, LinkedIn, YouTube, and email before creating anything new.
Commission one original data study per quarter — proprietary numbers that AI cannot paraphrase must be attributed to your brand, making this the highest-leverage citation-building activity.
Track AI brand mentions as a primary KPI — use tools like Profound to measure share of AI voice alongside traditional search share; "impressions up, pipeline flat" signals a broken strategy.
Prompt customers to leave Reddit reviews in NPS surveys and SMS campaigns — this generates authentic UGC that shifts both community sentiment and LLM training signals organically.



Personal Learning


Distribution is a compounding asset, not a one-time task. The brands that built Reddit and YouTube presence in 2024–2025 are now structurally advantaged in AI search — the window to build cheaply is closing.
AI visibility and search visibility are now the same problem. The Reddit threads ranking at the top of Google are the same threads feeding ChatGPT, Gemini, and Perplexity. A unified strategy across both is non-negotiable.
"Create Once, Distribute Forever" is not a content hack — it is an operating model. Simmonds applies it to his own business: one podcast episode becomes a newsletter, LinkedIn posts, Reddit threads, and YouTube clips. The system is the strategy.
