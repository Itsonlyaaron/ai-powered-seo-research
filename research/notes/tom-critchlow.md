Main Themes

-Tom's most consistent thread across all recent content is the repositioning of search as a brand and reputation discipline rather than a technical or tactical one. 

-His second major theme is the organizational transformation AI enables — not just efficiency gains, but throughput-level changes that rewrite how companies can operate.


Additional recurring themes:

-The executive skills gap in SEO and the need for search professionals to speak the language of business strategy

-The fragmentation of "AI search" across publisher, e-commerce, B2B SaaS, and consumer contexts

-The importance of authentic, human, third-party voices in shaping AI model outputs

-The arrival of agentic commerce and what it means for product discoverability




AI SEO Principles

-AEO is brand marketing, not technical SEO. The tactics (keyword research, on-page optimization) don't transfer. The strategy (reputation, authority, associations, community sentiment) does.

-How you show up > whether you show up. Every AI citation now carries implicit positive or negative valence — a brand cited as "don't buy from them" is worse than not being cited at all.

-AI search is a two-machine system. Training data (opaque, infrequent, community-sourced) and live retrieval (essentially structured Google searches) work together. Strategy must address both.

-Reputation is the algorithm. Reddit, forums, and authentic third-party voices are more powerful inputs to LLM outputs than on-site content optimization.

-Measurement must evolve. Replace keyword rank tracking with share of model voice, sentiment analysis, and brand characterization audits — metrics closer to brand measurement than SEO reporting.

-The whole organic pie is now in play. AI search puts all organic traffic at risk and in flux, which is why this problem has finally reached the C-suite in a way classical SEO never did.

-AI search fragments by business type. Publishers face a traffic and licensing problem. E-commerce faces a product discovery and agentic commerce problem. B2B SaaS faces a brand characterization and consideration problem. One-size strategies will fail.




Content Strategy Lessons

-Authentic human voices (influencers, internal experts, recognizable individuals) are becoming the highest-value AEO content assets.

-Self-serving listicles and comparison pages are losing credibility in AI outputs and represent a short-term tactic with long-term brand risk.

-Over-indexing on AI-generated content can cause long-term, potentially irreversible damage to how models characterize a brand.

-Linking out to quality external sources in content adds user value and signals the kind of trustworthiness that influences AI citation behavior.

-Substack and individual-author publishing formats carry special weight because they signal a verifiable real human behind the content.

-For enterprise content programs, custom tooling for AI-assisted content is essential — generic AI writing tools won't integrate cleanly into quality content workflows.




B2B SaaS Takeaways

-The B2B buying journey is increasingly research-heavy inside LLMs — a prospect may spend 20 minutes in ChatGPT or Perplexity before your rank tracker sees a single signal.

-For B2B, AI search characterization is essentially competitor analysis on autopilot: the model is deciding your shortlist position before a human ever visits your site.

-Brand PR, analyst relations, third-party review sites (G2, Capterra, Reddit), and expert community presence are the B2B AEO playbook.

-Thought leaders and named experts within a company significantly influence how LLMs characterize that brand in commercial contexts.

-SEO teams should stop trying to own the AEO outcome and instead become orchestrators of brand, PR, product, and community inputs that drive it.




Actionable Insights

-Audit your AI reputation, not just your AI visibility. Use tools like Profound, Peec AI, or manual LLM prompting to understand not just whether you're cited, but how you're characterized.

-Treat Reddit and community platforms as AEO infrastructure. Authentic community sentiment is one of the strongest inputs to LLM training data.

-Build a named-expert program. Humans, influencers, and identifiable authors carry disproportionate weight in AI output trustworthiness signals.

-Adopt a dual-track AI search strategy. Run a stable strategic workstream (enduring brand and authority signals) in parallel with a fast-moving experimentation workstream (new tools, workflows, opportunities).

-Brief executives using brand language, not SEO language. CMOs and CEOs understand "how does the AI describe us to a prospect?" — they don't understand keyword rank distributions.

-Don't silo AEO in the SEO team. Brand, PR, product, and community teams must be co-owners of AI search outcomes.

-Think in terms of throughput, not efficiency. AI's real organizational opportunity is doing 1,000 versions of something, not doing one thing 20% faster.
