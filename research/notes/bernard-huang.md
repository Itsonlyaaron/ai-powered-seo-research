Bernard Huang — Summary

Who is Bernard Huang?

Bernard Huang is the co-founder and CEO of Clearscope, the leading AI-powered SEO and content optimization platform trusted by enterprise teams at Adobe, HubSpot, Shopify, and Condé Nast. With over a decade of experience in SEO — including roles as a growth advisor at 500 Startups and growth lead at the YC-backed startup 42Floors — Bernard is one of the most respected voices on the intersection of AI and organic search strategy. He is particularly relevant to B2B SaaS marketers navigating the transition from traditional keyword-based SEO to Answer Engine Optimization (AEO) and Generative Engine Optimization (GEO) in an AI-first search environment.


Main Themes

SEO is dead; AI citation is the new organic visibility. Across all content, Bernard consistently argues that ranking in traditional blue links is no longer sufficient — brands must engineer their content and entity presence to be cited by ChatGPT, Gemini, and Perplexity when buyers ask relevant questions.
Judgment over scale: the real role of AI in content and product. Bernard challenges the prevailing "do more with less headcount" framing of AI, arguing that agents amplify expert judgment and raise the quality bar — meaning teams that invest in strategic depth will outperform those that merely automate volume.
Ownable niches beat broad authority. Recurring across his podcast appearances and content, Bernard emphasises that brands — especially smaller ones — win by going deep on specific, defensible topic lanes rather than chasing high-volume generalised keywords.



AI & Content Marketing Insights

The primary KPI for AI search is citation share of voice across target prompts — not keyword rankings. Teams need prompt tracking systems analogous to traditional rank trackers.
AI models operate via a three-layer retrieval stack (training data → validation searches → memory/context); content must be optimised to influence all three layers, not just on-page SEO signals.
Entity-based optimisation — clearly defining your brand, its relationships to industry concepts, and linking to authoritative external databases (Wikipedia, Wikidata, LinkedIn) — is the foundational AEO technical requirement.
AI cannot replicate first-hand experience, proprietary data, and trust-based perspectives — these are the durable content advantages in a landscape flooded with AI-generated commodity text.
AI agents are emerging as a multiplier on content team judgment, not a replacement — teams with sharp strategic frameworks will produce compounding output advantages over those treating AI as an automation layer alone.



Practical Takeaways for B2B SaaS

Build a prompt coverage map immediately. Identify every scenario, comparison, and use case your buyers might enter into ChatGPT, Gemini, or Perplexity during their decision process — then audit your brand's citation presence for each and prioritise content gaps.
Shift measurement to include AI visibility metrics. Supplement your existing SEO dashboard with brand mention rate across AI engines, AI platform referral traffic (Perplexity, ChatGPT, Google AI Mode), and share of voice versus named competitors on key prompts.
Invest in case study and use-case content over top-of-funnel informational articles. LLMs recommend brands that have documented, specific proof of solving customer problems — detailed case studies and scenario-specific solution pages are the AEO equivalent of high-DR backlinks.



Personal Learning

The measurement gap is the biggest strategic risk. Most B2B SaaS teams are still optimising for a metric — organic click-through traffic — that is rapidly declining in representativeness. The teams that will win are those that adopt AI visibility measurement now, before competitors force the issue.
Foundational SEO quality and AEO are not in tension. Bernard's repeated point that "good SEO is good GEO" is a crucial corrective to the industry hype around GEO-specific tactics — schema stuffing and llms.txt files are not the leverage point; content depth, entity clarity, and topical authority are.
AI agents change the nature of competitive advantage in SaaS. Bernard's framing that agents will bypass SaaS UI layers entirely is a strategic prompt for B2B SaaS marketers — content teams that build agent-accessible, structured, AEO-optimised content assets are also future-proofing product discoverability in agentic workflows.
