Podcast 1 — The Reddit Strategy That's Winning in 2026
Podcast Name: BuzzStream Podcast (Digital PR & Link Building)
Episode Title: The Reddit Strategy That's Winning in 2026 with Ross Simmonds
Date: February 25, 2026
Host: BuzzStream Team
URL: https://www.buzzstream.com/blog/reddit-marketing-podcast/
Short Summary: In this second appearance on the BuzzStream podcast, Simmonds focuses entirely on Reddit as a B2B growth engine in 2026. He explains why Reddit is better understood as a massive forum than a social network, and walks through the Listen → Map → Engage → Seed → Convert framework that Foundation uses for clients. He also addresses measurement — tracking Reddit-sourced pipeline, deal size, and sentiment shifts as the core ROI signals.

Key Insights:
Reddit functions as a forum, not a social network — brands that approach it with Twitter/LinkedIn instincts get banned; those that lurk, understand community norms, and add genuine value build durable influence.
Brands can encourage customers to leave reviews on Reddit (via NPS surveys, SMS campaigns) to generate authentic UGC that shifts community sentiment organically.
The primary ROI metrics for Reddit are pipeline influenced, deal size from Reddit-sourced leads, and CRM-tracked SQLs with Reddit as the source — not impressions or engagement.
Installing the Reddit pixel on pricing and competitor-comparison pages enables retargeting at 40–60% lower CPL than LinkedIn, making it the highest-ROI setup action for most B2B teams.

Podcast 2 — Smart Content Distribution Strategies
Podcast Name: The Agents of Change Digital Marketing Podcast
Episode Title: Smart Content Distribution Strategies with Ross Simmonds
Date: July 30, 2025
Host: Rich Brooks
URL: https://www.theagentsofchange.com/ross-simmonds
Short Summary: Simmonds explains why hitting "publish" is only the starting line, not the finish. He breaks down a repeatable framework for turning one piece of content into a multi-channel distribution asset, covering repurposing, resharing cadence, channel-content fit, and how to identify which existing content has been under-distributed. The episode is particularly focused on helping small and mid-sized businesses build systems, not one-off campaigns.

Key Insights:
Most brands are stuck in a creation loop ("create, create, create") without ever building the distribution infrastructure that would multiply each asset's reach.
"Channel-content fit" and "content-user fit" are distinct variables — the same piece needs to be reformatted (not just reposted) to perform well on different platforms.
Sales teams are an underutilised distribution channel: equipping them with specific content for specific objections is a high-leverage, low-cost play most B2B marketing teams ignore.
Identifying your top-performing content from the past 12–24 months and systematically redistributing it is faster and lower-risk than creating new content.

Podcast 3 — Ross Simmonds Show: SEO vs. AEO vs. GEO with Mike King
Podcast Name: The Ross Simmonds Show (HubSpot Podcast Network)
Episode Title: SEO vs. AEO vs. GEO: The Search Evolution You Can't Afford to Ignore (with Mike King, iPullRank)
Date: 2025 (available on Apple Podcasts, Spotify, iHeart)
Host: Ross Simmonds (note: this is Simmonds as host, not guest — included as a high-value recent episode from his own show)
URL: https://podcasts.apple.com/us/podcast/the-ross-simmonds-show/id1653650073
Short Summary: Simmonds interviews Mike King (founder of iPullRank) to dissect the evolution from traditional SEO to AEO (Answer Engine Optimisation) and GEO (Generative Engine Optimisation). They explore RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation) systems, the Google leak, and why the C-suite needs a different framing of "AI search" than the "it's just SEO" narrative that limits budget and strategic authority. The episode is a practical masterclass in framing GEO for leadership buy-in.

Key Insights:
Calling AI search "just SEO 2.0" undersells it to executives — the trillion-dollar narrative around ChatGPT and AI assistants is what unlocks C-suite budget and attention.
AI platforms evaluate an entire content ecosystem — not just a website — meaning off-domain assets (Reddit, YouTube, Medium, Quora) are now core SEO infrastructure.
RAG systems pull from authoritative, well-distributed sources; brands without a multi-surface presence are structurally invisible to AI retrievers even if they rank on Google.
Guest posts, podcasts, newsletters, and press releases build the narrative associations that shape AI "memory" of a brand — distributed storytelling is the new link building.
