Short Summary: Koray co-founded this weekly podcast with UK-based entrepreneur James Dooley to address the widening gap between mainstream SEO advice and how modern retrieval systems actually function. Episodes run 30–120 minutes, with Koray's segments frequently extending longer due to deep analysis of Google patents and information retrieval research. Available on Spotify, Apple Podcasts, Amazon Music, and YouTube.

Key Insights:

Modern search visibility is determined by machine understanding of entities, context, and semantic meaning — not keyword repetition.

Each episode deconstructs entity-attribute-value relationships (semantic triples) and how they influence query fan-out and ranking stability.

Traditional optimization fails when content lacks semantic depth; structured topical coverage is what enables search engines to infer expertise and trust at scale.

The podcast models the content strategy it teaches: structured, entity-first, consistently published with clear authorship signals.

Short Summary: A condensed transcript of an interview exploring how search engines evolve under LLM-driven retrieval systems, the continuing role of topical authority and semantic content networks, and why the principles of information retrieval and cost-of-retrieval reduction remain constant even as SERP features change. Koray also revisits the Dubai 2024 announcement of "Orchestra of Agents" — AI agent pipelines for automating semantic SEO workflows.

Key Insights:

AI Overviews alter click models but the underlying selection criterion — being a representative, low-cost-to-retrieve source — does not change.

"Orchestra of Agents" (announced Dubai 2024) is Koray's framework for deploying coordinated AI agents across the content creation and optimization pipeline.

Brand-driven demand is increasingly necessary because AI engines prefer entities with verifiable, historical, multi-source signals.

Cost-of-retrieval reduction means structuring, interlinking, and machine-reading-optimizing content so retrieval systems can parse it with minimal computational overhead.






Podcast 3 — Kalicube Summit 2026 (Session — "Cost of Retrieval & The Algorithmic Trinity")

Podcast Name: Kalicube Summit 2026 (Online Conference / On-Demand Sessions)

Episode Title: Cost-of-Retrieval Economics & Serving Dynamic Markdown to LLM Agents

Date: June 24, 2026

Host: Jason Barnard (Kalicube)

URL: https://kalicubesummit.com / https://www.eventbrite.com/e/the-kalicube-summit-2026-from-aeo-to-aao-tickets-1989277889718

Short Summary: At the Kalicube Summit 2026 (online, Day 1), Koray delivered two sessions covering the cost-of-retrieval economic model and its application to AI engine optimization. The key reveal: his team now serves dynamic markdown files customized to the retrieval preferences of individual LLM agents (ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity), recognizing that each system ingests and selects sources differently.

Key Insights:

Different LLMs have distinct retrieval behaviors — content must be adapted per agent, not served identically to all.

Serving structured markdown instead of rendered HTML reduces parsing overhead for AI crawlers, directly lowering cost of retrieval.

The "Algorithmic Trinity" (LLM intelligence + search information + knowledge graph verification) is the new three-layer stack that content must satisfy to gain AI citations.

Recency combined with accurate entity attribution is the mechanism that keeps a cited claim "alive" in LLM training and retrieval pipelines.
