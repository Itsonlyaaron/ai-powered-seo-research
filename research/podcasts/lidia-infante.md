-Volatility in localisation signals during core updates disproportionately affects international SEO — a critical watch area for global SaaS brands.




Podcast 2 — Strategy Sessions Podcast | "Dating Data with Lidia Infante"

Podcast Name: Strategy Sessions (Eximo Marketing)
Episode Title: Dating Data with Lidia Infante
Date: April 2024
Host: Andi Jarvis
URL: https://eximomarketingstrategy.com/dating-data-with-lidia-infante-strategy-sessions-podcast/

Short Summary: In a deliberately non-SEO episode, Lidia and host Andi Jarvis explore how marketing and data principles apply to personal decision-making — specifically the analytical logic behind selecting a life partner. The episode is both entertaining and intellectually rigorous, using the lens of marketing strategy (emotional + rational triggers) to discuss high-stakes personal choices. Lidia's psychology background shines throughout.

Key Insights:

-Rational data alone doesn't persuade — decisions are made when both the emotional and rational triggers are satisfied simultaneously, in SEO pitches as much as in life.

-To land an idea with a time-poor stakeholder, repetition and context matter more than one compelling stat.

-Psychology of perception and memory is foundational to effective marketing — understanding how people remember is as important as what you tell them.

-Framing your work around your stakeholder's cognitive load (not your own expertise) 
dramatically improves communication outcomes.




Podcast 3 — EDGE of the Web | Episodes 637 & 640 (Decoding User Intent / Optimising a Headless CMS)

Podcast Name: EDGE of the Web
Episode Title: 637 | Decoding User Intent w/ Lidia Infante (Singular vs. Plural Keywords) + 640 | Optimising a Headless CMS w/ Lidia Infante

Date: Late 2023 / Early 2024
Host: Erin Sparks
URL: https://edgeofthewebradio.com/marketing-concepts/lidia-infante/

Short Summary: In a two-part appearance, Lidia first tackled the nuanced difference in user intent between singular and plural keyword variants — a data-backed study she co-published with Search Engine Journal. In the second segment, she demystified headless CMS migration for SEOs, outlining the communication pitfalls that most often derail technically sound projects.

Key Insights:

-Singular and plural keyword variants signal meaningfully different user intents — this should be treated as a strategic signal, not a stylistic preference.

-GSC impression data is a practical and immediate way to determine which keyword variant to prioritise on large ecommerce sites.

-Headless CMS SEO failures are almost always communication failures between SEO and dev teams, not technical ones.

-Mapping your knowledge of the existing CMS structure before migration is a critical but consistently skipped step.
