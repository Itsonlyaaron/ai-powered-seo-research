Podcast 2 — Authority Hacker Podcast: We're Closing Our Courses (Pivoting to AI)

Podcast Name: Authority Hacker Podcast – AI & Automation for Small Biz & Marketers

Episode Title: We're Closing Our Multi-Million Dollar Course Business

Date: Late 2024 / Early 2025

Host: Gael Breton & Mark Webster (co-hosts)

URL: https://podcasts.apple.com/us/podcast/authority-hacker-podcast-ai-automation-for-small-biz/id1073349789

Short Summary: Breton and Webster openly discuss shutting down their flagship SEO course business (15,000+ customers) as a direct result of the collapse of the affiliate content site model post-HCU. They frame the pivot to AI and automation not as a defeat but as the only intellectually honest direction: the old template-driven SEO model is broken; systematised AI workflows are what actually drive revenue now.


Key Insights:

The "low-barrier, template-driven" affiliate site model is structurally over — Google's quality signals now require genuine brand authority.

Closing a profitable course business was the right call when the underlying tactics became unethical to teach at scale.

AI automation is not the next shiny object — it is the operational infrastructure for lean
 teams to compete with large ones.

Business owners must separate "SEO is dead" (false) from "affiliate content SEO is dead" (accurate) to chart the correct strategy.






Podcast 3 — Authority Hacker Podcast: Generative Engine Optimization (GEO) with Steve Toth

Podcast Name: Authority Hacker Podcast – AI & Automation for Small Biz & Marketers

Episode Title: GEO: How to Rank in ChatGPT and AI Chatbots (feat. Steve Toth)

Date: 2025

Host: Gael Breton & Mark Webster (co-hosts); Guest: Steve Toth (Notebook.agency)

URL: https://rss.buzzsprout.com/1885879.rss

Short Summary: Breton and Webster host Steve Toth for a deep dive into GEO — what it actually is, whether AI chatbot traffic converts better than organic search traffic, how to track your brand's presence inside LLMs, and the risk of "bloating" your site with AI-generated content in an attempt to game LLM retrieval. The consensus: GEO is real but early; brand narrative control and content structure matter most.


Key Insights:

AI chatbot traffic is currently high-intent and high-converting, but too low in volume to replace lost Google traffic at most business sizes.

Bloating a site specifically for AI retrieval is risky — LLMs penalise thin, repetitive content just as Google does.

Correcting misinformation about your brand in LLMs ("off-page GEO") is an emerging discipline that requires active monitoring.

Structuring content for AI retrieval (clear entity relationships, concise factual summaries, strong brand signals) overlaps heavily with E-E-A-T best practice.
