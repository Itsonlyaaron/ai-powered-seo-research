Title: SEO in the Age of AI — Kevin Indig on Google Overviews, E-Commerce & The Future of Search
Platform: YouTube / Page 2 Podcast
Host: Jon Clark and Joe DeVita
Published: September 15, 2025
Link: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qujABKOAThA
Summary

Indig revisits the structural shift in organic search driven by AI Overviews and Google's AI Mode, arguing that 2024 marked "peak traffic year" for organic click volume. He explains how B2B SaaS and e-commerce companies need different responses to the zero-click era, and warns against rebranding SEO as GEO without understanding what actually drives LLM citations.

Key Insights

2024 was peak traffic year. AI Overviews and AI Mode absorb top- and mid-funnel queries; the clicks that remain are high-intent and close to a purchase decision.
Traffic decoupling is real but not catastrophic for B2B. Clients are seeing organic traffic fall while demos and pipeline grow, because visitors who still click through are further along the buying journey.
AI Mode has a near-100% zero-click rate for informational queries. SEO's primary job is now to shape brand recognition before someone searches, not to intercept them mid-search.
B2B SaaS and e-commerce need different strategies. SaaS must build category authority and third-party citation breadth; e-commerce must fight for transactional visibility inside AI interfaces.
Directness, entity density, and freshness drive LLM citations. Indig warns against "GEO hysteria" — rebranding SEO without understanding these underlying citation signals is a distraction.

Title: Chat With Kevin Indig — The Great Decoupling, SEO Attribution & AI-Driven Growth Strategies
Platform: YouTube / Chat With Sean
Host: Sean Barber
Published: April 23, 2026
Link: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=p3_P0dDspBI

Summary

Indig introduces his "Great Decoupling" thesis — the idea that SEO has permanently separated from traffic as its primary output metric — and explores what this means for attribution, content strategy, and B2B growth. He outlines how brands winning in AI search are building topical authority maps aligned to ICP buyer journeys rather than chasing keyword volume.

Key Insights

The click-to-ranking relationship has broken. Brands that shifted from optimising for clicks to building authority are seeing pipeline growth despite traffic declines — the two metrics have decoupled.
Standard analytics tools cannot measure AI-era influence. GA4 and GSC dashboards miss influence that occurs inside LLM interfaces; teams need proxy models such as brand search volume, dark social signals, and sales team feedback.
LLMs reward ICP specificity, not keyword breadth. Content aligned to precise buyer questions outperforms broad evergreen content in AI citation probability.
Third-party validation outweighs high-DA backlinks. Analyst mentions, G2 reviews, and community citations are now stronger inputs to AI citation probability than domain authority alone.
Build a topical authority map before producing more content. This framework defines which problems, use cases, and buyer stages the brand needs to own conceptually — across the website, LinkedIn, and third-party publications.
