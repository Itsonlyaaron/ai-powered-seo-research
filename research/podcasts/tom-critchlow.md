Podcast 1 — Content and Conversation (Siege Media)

Podcast Name: Content and Conversation: Organic Growth Insights from Siege Media

Episode Title: What Your CMO Needs to Know About AI Search

Date: June 2026

Host: Ross Hudgens

URL: https://podcasts.apple.com/us/podcast/content-and-conversation-organic-growth-insights-from/id1289467174

Short Summary: Ross Hudgens and Tom Critchlow cover what GEO actually is in 2026, why measurement looks more like brand tracking than SEO reporting, and what the "yes stance" looks like for SEOs navigating executive conversations. The episode addresses technical SEO in the age of model distillation, NavBoost and clicks as trust signals, and the personalized push-not-pull future of search discovery.


Key Insights:

-SEOs cannot afford to be defensive about AI search — the posture shift from "SEO is fine" to "yes, and here's how we adapt" is a career-defining move.

-GEO measurement should include share of model, share of voice in AI outputs, and sentiment analysis — not just citation counts.

-NavBoost and user click signals remain important trust inputs even in AI-mediated search environments.

-Technical SEO in the model distillation era still matters, but its leverage points are shifting toward structured data, entity clarity, and content grounding quality.






Podcast 2 — The Search Session (Advanced Web Ranking)

Podcast Name: The Search Session

Episode Title: Leveling Up SEO for the AI Era

Date: October 20, 2025

Host: Gianluca Fiorelli

URL: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dNUG39V6LWk (also available: https://www.advancedwebranking.com/blog/leveling-up-seo-in-ai-era)

Short Summary: Tom unpacks uncomfortable truths about modern SEO and how search professionals can move from tactical executors to genuine business leaders in the AI era. He addresses the fractured relationship between the search industry and Google post-Helpful Content Update, the role of links in an AI-dominated interface, and why real SEO wins come from brand, product, and PR — not classic SEO tactics.


Key Insights:

-At scale, SEO lives inside product teams as multiple specialties — and AI will accelerate this fragmentation further.

-AI search is "stuck in a wall of text"; linking out to quality external sources is a way to restore real user value and citation trust.

-Google Web Guide points toward the future of search results — LLM-led, structured, link-rich — though real-time delivery speed remains a barrier.

-The uncomfortable truth for SEOs: real wins come from brand, product, and PR investments ,SEO teams mostly diagnose, while other teams execute the fixes that matter.






Podcast 3 — BRXND Dispatch (Mike Mallazzo / Alephic)

Podcast Name: BRXND Dispatch

Episode Title: On AI Search with Tom Critchlow of Alephic — Vol. 116

Date: June 4, 2026

Host: Mike Mallazzo

URL: https://newsletter.brxnd.ai/p/on-ai-search-with-tom-critchlow-of

Short Summary: Mike Mallazzo's in-depth interview marking Tom's entry to Alephic, covering why AEO is constitutionally different from SEO, how AI retrieval really works under the hood, the importance of reputation over optimization, and why the entire organic search pie is now up for grabs. Tom introduces the two-machine framework for AI retrieval (training data vs. live retrieval) and the role of community platforms like Reddit and Substack as trust amplifiers.


Key Insights:

-The binary ranking model of SEO (you rank or you don't) has been replaced by a qualitative model — brands can be cited negatively, neutrally, or positively, and all three produce radically different outcomes.

-AI search surfaces reputation as code: community sentiment on Reddit, forums, and social platforms now directly shapes LLM outputs, often in training data.

-Brands should think about "known voices and recognizable individuals" — influencers, internal experts, real humans — as their most powerful AEO assets.

-Agentic commerce (where software agents make or influence purchase decisions) represents the next frontier, requiring brands to think about machine-readable product information, reviews, and inventory data in entirely new ways.
