Podcast 1
Podcast Name: saas.unbound (by saas.group)
Episode Title: AI-driven SEO Revolution: The Future of Discoverability with Bernard Huang @Clearscope
Date: November 18, 2025
Host: Anna Nadeina
URL: https://saas.group/podcasts/ai%E2%80%91driven-seo-revolution-the-future-of-discoverability-with-bernard-huang-clearscope/
Short Summary: Bernard recounts his journey from growth-hacking SEO tactics at the YC startup 42Floors to building Clearscope as AI fundamentally rewired search. He explains the three-layer model of AI retrieval — training data, validation search, and memory/context — and provides a concrete framework for B2B SaaS brands to win citations across all three layers.

Key Insights:
The shift from "ranking in blue links" to "influencing the AI answer" is the defining reframe every content team needs to internalize in 2025–2026.
AI citation pages must be actively maintained with current positioning, differentiators, and examples — because an AI citing an outdated or vague page will represent your brand poorly.
For B2B SaaS, the highest-leverage AEO content targets the specific use-case scenarios buyers search for in AI tools, not broad informational keywords.
Short-term GEO manipulation tactics (schema stuffing, inauthentic mentions) produce temporary gains and accumulate model distrust risk — durability comes from genuine usefulness.



Podcast 2
Podcast Name: Siege Media Podcast / Content & Conversation
Episode Title: How GEO Actually Works w/ Bernard Huang
Date: January 14, 2026
Host: Ross Hudgens (CEO, Siege Media)
URL: https://www.siegemedia.com/conversation/how-geo-actually-works-w-bernard-huang
Short Summary: Bernard returns to the Siege Media podcast to debate whether GEO is truly a new discipline or simply SEO evolving under new conditions. The conversation covers how AI agents process queries differently from keyword-based retrieval, the collapse of top-funnel traffic from AI Mode becoming the default search experience, and how to measure success when traditional rank tracking no longer captures the full picture.

Key Insights:
Bernard's core thesis: "good SEO is good GEO" — the fundamentals of earning trust, covering topics comprehensively, and building brand authority translate directly to AI citation eligibility.
The shift from backlinks to brand mentions as an influence signal is underway — AI models weight named entity mentions and citation patterns differently than PageRank logic.
Measuring GEO success requires tracking impressions, AI citations, and brand search volume alongside traditional clicks — and accepting that "zero-click visibility" has real strategic value.
The "long, long tail" of conversational AI queries represents a massive content surface area — brands that map and serve niche use-case scenarios will accumulate citation share others miss.



Podcast 3
Podcast Name: Content Disrupted (by Skyword)
Episode Title: SEO in the Age of Generative AI: Mastering Topical Authority and Content Strategy with Bernard Huang
Date: 2025 (published/updated May 2026)
Host: Dan Baptiste (EVP, Skyword)
URL: https://www.skyword.com/contentstandard/seo-in-the-age-of-generative-ai-mastering-topical-authority-and-content-strategy-with-bernard-huang/
Short Summary: Bernard and Skyword EVP Dan Baptiste explore how generative AI is cannibalising informational search traffic and what brands must do to adapt. The conversation covers the new meaning of topical authority, the importance of "ownable lanes," and why trust-based content (reviews, first-hand perspectives, expert recommendations) is what AI cannot replace.

Key Insights:
AI excels at consensus-based, structured answers (recipes, templates, itineraries) — content teams should cede those queries and focus where first-hand experience and trust are irreplaceable differentiators.
Brands should identify a narrow "ownable lane" — a topic niche deeply aligned with brand identity — and build multichannel authority there rather than competing on broad, high-volume terms.
Search has become a mid-to-bottom-of-funnel channel; discovery now happens on social and AI interfaces, so content strategies must be designed accordingly.
Smaller publishers can outrank larger competitors by going granular — deep, nuanced content on specific subtopics accumulates topical authority that aggregate high-volume pages cannot match.
