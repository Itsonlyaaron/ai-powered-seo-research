Post 1 — SEO is Inherently Cross-Functional

Date: Early 2025 

Topic: Cross-functional nature of SEO within organisations

Original Link: https://www.linkedin.com/post/ lidiainfante_seo-is-inherently-crossfunctional-its-a-activity-7293057937143463936-u8pl

Short Summary: Lidia argues that SEO's core challenge is not technical but organisational — it depends on buy-in from product, engineering, content, and marketing teams. Without cross-functional alignment, even the best SEO strategy stalls. The post challenges the idea that SEO is a siloed discipline.

Key Insights:

-SEO cannot succeed as a lone-team function — it is structurally dependent on input from product, content, dev, and brand teams. 

-The biggest barrier to SEO execution is internal alignment, not external algorithm changes. 

-SEO leaders must act as project managers and communicators as much as technical practitioners. 

-Framing SEO as a revenue channel (rather than a traffic channel) is key to getting stakeholder buy-in.





Post 2 — Internationalisation Is Also a Technology Problem

Date: 2025 

Topic: International SEO beyond language and culture — the technology layer

Original Link: https://www.linkedin.com/in/lidiainfante/

Short Summary: Lidia highlights a common blind spot in international SEO: most practitioners focus on language and cultural adaptation but overlook the technological infrastructure that enables internationalisation at scale. She notes there are established benchmarks for this, but they are widely ignored. The post encourages SEOs to audit their tech stack before expanding internationally.

Key Insights:

-Internationalisation failures are often technology failures, not content failures.
Benchmarks exist for international tech SEO but are underused 

-practitioners should start there. Treating hreflang as a complete international SEO strategy is insufficient on its own.

-True international SEO requires coordination across localisation, engineering, and product teams.






Post 3 — The State of Learning in SEO (2025 Study Launch)

Date: August–September 2025 (tied to blog post publication: August 31, 2025)

Topic: How SEOs learn in 2025; role of newsletters, events, and social media

Original Link: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/lidiainfante_datascience-seo-wearesurveymonkey-activity-7346551483066163202-ho8b

Short Summary: Lidia launched a study on how the SEO industry learns following the decline of Twitter/X as a knowledge-sharing hub. The data reveals that newsletters are now rated the most effective learning resource by practitioners, while live events underperform expectations. The findings have practical implications for where SEOs should invest their learning time.

Key Insights:

-Newsletters (78% effectiveness rating) and specialist industry sites (72% usage) now dominate SEO learning — events rank lowest.

-The collapse of Twitter as a centralised industry forum has fragmented professional knowledge-sharing across LinkedIn, Slack, and newsletters.

-SEOFOMO is the industry's favourite newsletter; BrightonSEO is the most popular in-person event.

-Gender and seniority influence learning habits — a dimension often overlooked in industry studies.
