Post 1 — The Citation Gap: Why AI Mentions Your Brand But Doesn't Drive Traffic

Date: June 2026 (approx.)
Topic: AI search visibility, Citation Gap, GEO tactics
Original Link: https://www.linkedin.com/in/rosssimmonds/
Short Summary: Simmonds introduces the concept of the "Citation Gap" — a scenario where a brand is mentioned by ChatGPT but receives zero referral clicks because ChatGPT resolves queries without linking out. He contrasts ChatGPT (a "storyteller" with only ~12% of answers containing links) against Perplexity (a "librarian" where ~95% of answers link out). He argues that brands must optimise for both behaviours with different content formats.

Key Insights:
Only 12% of ChatGPT answers include links; it is designed to be the endpoint, not a referral channel. Perplexity is the primary AI traffic driver.
To earn links from AI, create proprietary data, original reports, and unique case studies — AI cites sources only when it cannot paraphrase the specificity.
Use structured formats (tables, numbered lists, graphs with clear headings) to increase extractability for AI systems.
Approximately 70% of brand mentions in AI conversations in 2026 are "dark" — appearing in chat sessions that generate no analytics signal.

Post 2 — B2B Buyers on Reddit & YouTube: Stop Treating Them as Side Projects
Date: April 12, 2026
Topic: B2B content strategy, Reddit, YouTube, AI citations
Original Link: https://www.linkedin.com/in/rosssimmonds/ (originally posted Apr 12, 2026)
Short Summary: Simmonds argues that Reddit and YouTube have evolved from peripheral channels into primary hubs for B2B buyer research. He points out that LLMs now frequently cite YouTube in AI-generated answers, and that Reddit dominates bottom-of-funnel commercial queries. He calls out B2B teams that treat these platforms as "side projects" and urges a shift toward problem-solving content published where buyers already ask questions.

Key Insights:
B2B buyer-intent queries — especially "best software" comparisons — are increasingly concentrated on Reddit, not branded websites.
YouTube is now regularly cited in LLM outputs, making it a key channel for both human discovery and AI training.
Publishing problem-solving content where buyers already congregate outperforms pushing content from your own domain and hoping it ranks.
Consistency matters: regular, high-value content on these platforms compounds authority over time for both algorithmic and AI visibility.

Post 3 — Traffic to Influence: The Biggest Marketing Shift of 2025–2026
Date: Mid-2025 / Early 2026 (multiple related posts)
Topic: AI search, brand influence, GEO, marketing philosophy
Original Link: https://ca.linkedin.com/in/rosssimmonds
Short Summary: Simmonds frames the current era as a shift from "traffic" to "influence" — visibility is no longer about click volume but about being cited, referenced, and recalled by AI systems and buyers who never visit your site. He argues that B2B brands must now optimise for share of voice inside AI conversations, not just Google rankings. He notes that by 2026, digital marketing has changed at a pace that makes even 2024 tactics feel outdated.

Key Insights:
The KPI shift: "impressions up, pipeline flat" signals a broken strategy — influence must connect to revenue, not just reach.
AI conversations reset constantly; a brand's visibility in AI answers is a probability field that must be continuously reinforced through distribution.
Domain strategy is an underrated SEO variable — regional domains (e.g., .ca) can silently cap international growth by anchoring visibility to a home market.
Content automation removes slow, repetitive tasks (drafting, formatting) but the human role — creating insightful, emotionally resonant stories — becomes more important as AI floods the web with mediocre content.
