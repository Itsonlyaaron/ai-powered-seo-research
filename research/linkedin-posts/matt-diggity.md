Post 1 — AI Visibility & Editorial Media (June 2026)

Date: June 2026 (recent, confirmed via LinkedIn profile feed)

Topic: AI Search Visibility — why brands are invisible in AI results and how to fix it

Original Link: https://www.linkedin.com/in/mattdiggityseo/

Short Summary: Matt audited a client's presence in AI search engines and found them invisible despite solid Google rankings. He diagnosed the root cause: 61% of what AI tools draw from is editorial media — press mentions, third-party coverage, and brand references across the broader web. Rankings alone no longer guarantee visibility.


Key Insights:

-Editorial/earned media now accounts for 61% of what AI systems surface in their responses — it is the dominant source, not your own website.

-Ranking #1 on Google does not guarantee visibility in ChatGPT, Perplexity, or Google AI Overviews — the discovery layer has shifted.

-Brands that invest in digital PR and getting independently mentioned across the web will outperform those focused purely on on-site SEO.

-AI search visibility is a brand reputation problem as much as a technical SEO problem.






Post 2 — Technical SEO for Startups (June 2026)

Date: ~June 2026 (LinkedIn feed, confirmed recent)

Topic: Technical SEO fundamentals for Series A startups wasting spend on paid ads

Original Link: https://th.linkedin.com/in/mattdiggityseo

Short Summary: Matt shared an audit of a $10M-funded startup with 500+ indexed pages generating only 200 organic visitors per month while spending $50k/month on paid ads. After fixing core technical SEO in 30 days, organic traffic jumped to 1,000+/month and 
paid ad dependency dropped 40%.


Key Insights:

-Developers building for users often ignore search engine fundamentals: mismatched page titles, broken internal linking, and content misaligned with buyer keywords.

-Technical SEO is a high-ROI lever for startups — the 30-day fix in this case quintupled organic traffic.

-Over-investment in paid ads is often a symptom of unfixed technical SEO, not a growth strategy.

-Beautiful websites are not findable websites — aesthetics and search-engine architecture are separate disciplines.






Post 3 — SEO in 2025 Will Be Unrecognizable (late 2024)

Date: December 2024

Topic: How the SEO landscape is being transformed by AI and shifting Google behavior

Original Link: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/mattdiggityseo_seo-in-2025-is-going-to-be-unrecognizable-activity-7275045912282836992-n2DC

Short Summary: Matt argued that 2025 will mark a structural break in SEO — not an incremental update cycle. Brand search volume, traffic diversity, and AI-readiness are becoming the dominant ranking signals, while old playbooks built around pure content 
volume and link counts are losing reliability.


Key Insights:

-Brand search volume is emerging as a critical ranking signal — sites without brand equity are being outperformed even with strong backlink profiles.

-Traffic diversity (social, paid, referral, direct) signals site legitimacy to Google; pure SEO-play sites are increasingly penalized or deprioritized.

-The SEO playbook requires rebuilding, not just updating — 2025 is a structural shift, not another update cycle.

-Sites recovering from Google's Helpful Content Update are those that invested in brand recognition and multi-channel presence.
