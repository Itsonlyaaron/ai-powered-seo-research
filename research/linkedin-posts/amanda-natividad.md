post I
Topic: Surviving the drop in Google click-through rates.
Date: Published June 2026
url: https://www.linkedin.com/feed/update/urn:li:activity:7470490713688850432/

summary: 68% of Google searches now end without a click. Instead of panicking, marketers need to adapt because "the click was never the point. It was just the thing we could measure." The new goal is to build brand affinity so people and AI recommend you by name.

Key InsightsThe "Traffic Scoreboard" is Broken:
 As of early 2026, 68% of Google searches end without a click. Generative AI, social platform algorithms, and instant answers actively keep users inside their own walled gardens.  
 The Click Was Always a Proxy: Marketers became obsessed with clicks because they were easy to measure, not because they were the ultimate goal. The true goal has always been brand affinity, trust, and revenue.
 AI Traffic is Low Volume, High Intent: Visitors referred by AI tools (like ChatGPT or Google's AI Overviews) arrive in lower numbers, but data shows they convert at a significantly higher rate because the machine has already helped them do the initial research.
 Brand is the Ultimate Moat: People and AI engines preferentially cite entities they recognize. When users search for your brand by name, it bypasses algorithmic gatekeeping and directly results in clicks.

 Date: June 23, 2026  
  Topic: AI in SEO and Brand VisibilityOriginal
Link: https://amandanat.substack.com/Short 
 
  Summary: Amanda analyzes HubSpot's AEO Grader and its assessment of SparkToro's digital presence. She argues that instead of obsessing over a singular "AI visibility score," marketers should view AI search engines as mirrors reflecting the actual strength and spread of their brand's footprint across the web. 
  
  Key Insights:
  AI search engines synthesize what is already being said about you; they don't invent authority out of thin air."Blind spots" in AI evaluations are often more useful than the high scores, as they reveal actual gaps in your content strategy.Optimizing for AI requires a holistic brand footprint rather than traditional keyword stuffing.

  Topic: B2B SaaS Marketing PsychologyOriginal 
  Link: https://amandanat.substack.com/Short 
 
  Summary: Amanda challenges the traditional notion that B2B purchases are purely logical while B2C purchases are emotional. She argues that because the stakes are significantly higher in B2B (e.g., risking your job or company budget on a software choice), the emotional weight of the decision is actually much greater. 
  
   Key Insights:
   B2B marketing must address fear, risk, and professional reputation, not just product features.Trust and brand affinity play a massive role in B2B conversions because buyers need emotional reassurance.Marketers should shift from dry, feature-heavy messaging to empathetic, risk-mitigating narratives.
