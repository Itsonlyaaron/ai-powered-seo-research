Post 1: Future of AI Search – Agentic Optimization

Date: January 28, 2026

Topic: AI agents, e-commerce, search optimization paradigm shift

Original Link: https://searchengineland.com/ai-search-visibility-seo-predictions-2026-468042

Summary:

Crystal positioned AI agents as the next frontier in search, warning that traditional "being found" strategies are obsolete. She emphasized that brands must optimize for machine-readability and real-time API compatibility as AI agents increasingly handle transactions without human involvement. The agentic web represents a fundamental shift from visibility to usability.


Key Insights:


AI agents will handle checkout, payment, and purchasing decisions without users leaving the conversation

Product, pricing, and inventory data must be machine-readable in real-time or competitors will dominate

Marketing funnel paradigm shifts when AI becomes a decision gatekeeper between brand and customer

SEO must now split into two distinct jobs: driving human clicks and supplying trusted inputs for AI agents



Post 2: Prompt Tracking as the New KPI

Date: March 2026 (Mayday article reference)

Topic: LLM visibility, citation tracking, KPI evolution

Original Link: https://searchengineland.com/ai-search-visibility-seo-predictions-2026-468042

Summary:

Crystal introduced "prompt tracking" as a replacement for traditional click-through metrics, arguing that visibility in AI tools doesn't always generate site traffic but signals brand authority. She explained how to monitor where your brand appears in LLM responses and why citations matter differently across platforms like ChatGPT, Gemini, and Perplexity.


Key Insights:


Traditional SEO KPIs (clicks, impressions) are insufficient for measuring AI search success

Prompt tracking reveals how often brands are mentioned in LLM responses, even without click-through

Different LLMs have different citation preferences (Gemini and ChatGPT favor Forbes; Claude values other sources)

Entity relationships matter more than keywords; LLMs understand contextual business associations



Post 3: Decentralized Search and Brand Entity Control

Date: February-March 2026

Topic: Brand entities, knowledge graph optimization, multi-platform search

Original Link: https://majestic.com/seo-in-2025/crystal-carter

Summary:

Crystal emphasized that search has become "dissipated and decentralized" across Google, Bing, ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude, and Gemini. She outlined a tactical strategy for controlling brand entity presence across the knowledge graph, including schema markup, Wikipedia alignment, and relationship-building within LLM ecosystems.


Key Insights:


Brand entity clarity across Wikipedia, Wikidata, schema markup, and LinkedIn is foundational for LLM visibility

Small brands can compete by building strong link relationships with entities that identify them

AI overviews and Copilot pull entity relationships from knowledge graphs; consistency is critical

Multimodal optimization (video, text, structured data) increases visibility across AI and traditional search
