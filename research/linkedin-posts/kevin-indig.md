Topic: Client questions about AI Mode and how it changes organic strategy.
Key Takeaways:

All of Indig's clients have asked him about AI Mode — signalling it has crossed from experimentation to a mainstream strategic concern.
He views 2024 as "peak traffic year" — future organic clicks will be a fraction of today's volume, but higher-intent.
Visibility in AI-generated answers (retrieval) is the new success metric, not raw page views or click volume.
Brands must build influence inside AI platforms (ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity) rather than depending on click-throughs to their own site.
Evergreen content formats (guides, tutorials, explainers) are becoming obsolete under AI Mode; content strategy must evolve toward authority signals and brand presence.



Post 2 — Operationalising Topic-First SEO in 5 Steps
Topic: A practical framework for shifting from keyword-centric SEO to topic-led content strategy
URL: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/kevinindig_operationalising-topic-first-seo-in-5-steps-activity-7350975378233954305-1hpW
Date: May–June 2025 (approx.)
Key Takeaways:

Topic maps — not keyword lists — should be the starting point for content planning; keywords live inside topic maps, not the other way around.
AI search indexes intent clusters, not individual pages, making topic coverage more important than individual keyword targeting.
The framework moves from: core topic identification → intent mapping → keyword measurement → content creation → performance review.
This approach aligns SEO with how AI search engines (Google Gemini, ChatGPT) synthesise multi-intent queries into a single response.
Companies that adopt topic-first SEO earlier will build broader entity authority, making them more retrievable across AI platforms.
