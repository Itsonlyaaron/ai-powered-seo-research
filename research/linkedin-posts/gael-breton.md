Post 1 — GEO/LLMO Is Still Just SEO (Reframed)

Date: 2025/2026 (recent, exact date not publicly confirmed)

Topic: AI-driven search, LLM optimization, information retrieval principles

Original Link: https://www.linkedin.com/in/gael-breton-78305118/

Short Summary: Breton pushes back on the idea that GEO, AEO, and LLMO are distinct from SEO, arguing that roughly 95% of what is needed to perform well in LLMs maps directly to fundamental SEO principles — specifically information retrieval, entities, and topical authority. He cautions that practitioners who abandon traditional SEO in favour of LLM-specific tactics will underperform those who deepen their understanding of entity-driven search.


Key Insights:

SEO in an AI-driven world is primarily about information retrieval and entity strength — not keyword tactics.

Topical authority and brand strength are the levers that influence LLM citation and ranking.

There are nuances to LLMs that require specific work, but pivoting your entire strategy away from SEO fundamentals is a mistake.

A correct framing: "Is SEO dead or has it expanded?" — it has expanded, and the core principles still hold.






Post 2 — AI SEO in 2026 Is Simple (Satirical Reality Check)

Date: 2026 (recent LinkedIn post)

Topic: The overloaded demands placed on modern SEO practitioners; AI search complexity

Original Link: https://www.linkedin.com/in/gael-breton-78305118/

Short Summary: A satirical post listing everything a piece of content must now simultaneously achieve — satisfy Google, impress AI models, pass E-E-A-T, stay semantically connected, and rank in AI Overviews — while keeping the author sane. The humour masks a sharp underlying point: the requirements placed on SEO have become operationally absurd without systematic AI-assisted workflows.


Key Insights:

Modern SEO combines technical, semantic, and brand signals that no manual workflow can juggle alone — AI assistance is no longer optional.

Google's "unhelpful" label is an existential threat to sites that fail on any one of multiple simultaneous dimensions.

Workflow automation is now the real competitive moat, not any single tactical insight.

Consistent, human-edited content output remains the practical solution, not obsessing over every new requirement.






Post 3 — AI Agents Are the New Crypto

Date: 2025 (LinkedIn post)

Topic: AI agent hype cycle; critical evaluation of AI tooling

Original Link: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/gael-breton-78305118_ai-agents-are-the-new-crypto-everyones-activity-7364628429738070016-GE6e

Short Summary: Breton compares AI agent hype to the crypto hype cycle, warning that most "agent" products are superficial wrappers rather than genuine business systems. He advocates for building agents that solve concrete, repeatable business tasks rather than chasing the trend.


Key Insights:

Most "AI agents" on the market are marketing narratives, not production-grade systems.

The value of AI automation comes from solving specific, high-frequency tasks — not general-purpose agents.

Building sales-enablement tools that are not easily duplicatable in ChatGPT or Gemini is a meaningful differentiator.

Marketers should evaluate AI tools against concrete ROI, not benchmark comparisons.
