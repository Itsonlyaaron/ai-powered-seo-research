Post 1 — Cost of Retrieval & LLM-Specific Content Serving

Date: June 2026 (pre-Kalicube Summit 2026 announcement post)

Topic: Cost-of-retrieval economics for LLMs and the "Algorithmic Trinity" (LLM intelligence + search information + knowledge graph verification)

Original Link: https://www.linkedin.com/in/koray-tugberk-gubur/

Short Summary: Koray announced his two sessions at the Kalicube Summit 2026, where he revealed that his team is now serving dynamic markdown files tailored to individual LLM agents — because ChatGPT, Claude, and Perplexity retrieve content differently. He frames "cost of retrieval" as the deciding variable: if a system already has a source satisfying a topic, it has no commercial incentive to find another, making quality alone insufficient.

Key Insights:

Quality of content is not enough — you must lower your cost of retrieval to compete for LLM citations.

The "Algorithmic Trinity" combines LLM intelligence, search information, and knowledge-graph verification; optimizing for all three is required.

ChatGPT, Claude, and Perplexity have distinct retrieval preferences — one-size-fits-all content fails across AI engines.

Recency is the mechanism that keeps attributed claims alive in LLM memory; publishing cadence matters for AI visibility.

Post 3 — Domain Rating Is Misleading: Topical Authority Is Structural

Date: Early 2026

Topic: Topical authority vs. Domain Rating as a ranking proxy

\Original Link: https://tr.linkedin.com/in/koray-tugberk-gubur/en

Short Summary: Koray argued that Domain Rating (DR) is a backlink metric, not a topical standing metric, and that treating DR as a proxy for authority causes SEOs to chase links when the real gap is content coverage. His formula — Historical Data × Topical Coverage — is the true determinant, meaning a low-DR domain can outrank a high-DR competitor by owning its topic.

Key Insights:

DR measures your backlink graph. Topical authority measures the fit between what your site covers and what a topic demands.

A site can reach topical dominance in the low DR teens if it maintains comprehensive coverage and publishing history.

Coverage without internal linking reads as "a pile of pages"; coverage with internal linking reads as a topic — internal links are the arteries of topical PageRank.

Link-building campaigns that substitute for content coverage will not fix a structural topical gap.
