Post 1
Topic: AI Agents as Judgment Multipliers, Not Headcount Replacements
Date: June 2025 (approx.)
Original Link: https://www.linkedin.com/in/bernardjhuang/ (post visible on profile feed)
Short Summary: Bernard challenges the dominant narrative that AI agents exist to reduce headcount, arguing they are instead multipliers of human judgment. He shares his personal experience running four active agents (Hermes, OpenClaw, Claude Code, Codex) and observes they intensified his workload rather than reducing it — because higher-quality judgment is now what determines outcomes.

Key Insights:
AI agents demand more decision-making from operators, not less — they surface complexity rather than hide it.
The real value of agents is leverage on expertise: they execute faster, but they rely on sharp human direction to produce useful outputs.
The SaaS model itself is at risk — as agents complete "jobs to be done" directly, the UI layer of many software products becomes redundant.
Founders and content teams should think of agent stacks as an amplification of their existing strategic edge, not a shortcut around building one.



Post 2
Topic: 2025 Was a Wild Year for SEO — What's Actually Changed
Date: Late 2025 / Early 2026
Original Link: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/bernardjhuang_2025-has-been-a-wild-year-for-seo-ai-overviews-activity-7403832519269535744-gZl2
Short Summary: Bernard reflects on the seismic shifts of 2025 for the SEO industry, covering the rise of AI Overviews, AI Mode becoming default on Google, and the collapse of traditional top-of-funnel informational traffic. He frames 2025 as the year when organic traffic patterns became permanently bifurcated between traditional and AI-mediated search.

Key Insights:
AI Overviews and Google's AI Mode have fundamentally altered how informational queries are resolved — many no longer produce organic clicks.
Top-of-funnel SEO traffic is not recovering; teams still anchored to volume-based keyword strategies are structurally disadvantaged.
Brand visibility in AI-generated answers is now a primary metric alongside traditional rank tracking.
Search has shifted toward a mid- to bottom-of-funnel channel, while discovery now happens on social and AI-chat interfaces.



Post 3
Topic: AEO Prompt Tracking — The New Equivalent of Keyword Tracking
Date: 2025
Original Link: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/bernardjhuang_if-seo-tracking-keywords-then-aeo-tracking-activity-7424551520656039936-Lhe1
Short Summary: Bernard draws a direct parallel between traditional keyword rank tracking and what AEO now requires: prompt tracking. In the same way SEO teams once monitored SERP positions for target keywords, AEO-focused teams must now systematically monitor AI engine responses to prompts relevant to their brand, category, and use cases.

Key Insights:
The AEO equivalent of a "keyword" is the prompt — and tracking brand citations across AI engines is the new rank monitoring discipline.
Teams should identify the complete scenario map of prompts their buyers might use and audit brand presence across ChatGPT, Gemini, and Perplexity for each.
Citation frequency and share of voice across AI engine responses are the core KPIs replacing traditional position 1–10 rankings.
Early movers who build prompt tracking systems now will have compounding data advantages over competitors who wait.
