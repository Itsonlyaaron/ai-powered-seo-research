Post 1 — Joining Alephic + AEO as Brand Marketing

Date: June 4–8, 2026

Topic: Career move to Alephic; AI search strategy as brand marketing vs. SEO tactic

Original Link: https://www.linkedin.com/in/tomcritchlow/

Short Summary: Tom announced his move from Raptive to Alephic as a Forward-Deployed Engineer, sharing the BRXND Dispatch interview in which he articulated his central thesis: AEO (AI Engine Optimization) is fundamentally brand marketing. He noted that "how you show up" in AI search responses now matters as much as whether you show up at all, and that most current AI retrieval is essentially Google searches wrapped in a conversational interface.

Key Insights:

-AEO is a shift from whether a brand shows up to how it shows up — quality of mention now determines conversion outcomes.

-Most current AI agent retrieval is "just Google searches in a trench coat," but that will evolve fast.

-Reputation, third-party voices (Reddit, real users), and authentic community content shape LLM model outputs more than on-site optimization.

-The work AI search unlocks for marketers is strategy, not optimization — a fundamentally more valuable category of engagement.






Post 2 — "Of Termites & Tokens": Rethinking AI Workflow Automation

Date: June 8, 2026

Topic: AI workflow automation and the shift from efficiency to throughput

Original Link: https://substack.com/@tomcritchlow/note/c-272851776

Short Summary: Tom shared his new blog post arguing that most corporate AI discourse around workflow automation is misguided. Instead of framing AI as a tool to "replace expensive humans with cheap tokens," he proposes thinking about what happens when AI changes the throughput of a workflow entirely — making it possible to run thousands of experiments, analyses, or creative outputs where teams previously ran one.

Key Insights:

-AI's real opportunity isn't efficiency (doing the same things cheaper) but throughput (doing fundamentally more things at once).

-The "termite colony" metaphor: distributed, autonomous AI agents acting at scale can reshape how organizations function — not as automation, but as a new organizational layer.

-Executives who only think about AI cost savings are missing the strategic transformation opportunity.

-For marketing organizations, this means moving from one campaign to continuous content experimentation at scale.






Post 3 — AI Search Strategy: Stable Foundation + Experimentation Workstream

Date: June 2026

Topic: How brands should approach AI search strategy operationally

Original Link: https://www.linkedin.com/in/tomcritchlow/

Short Summary: Tom outlined a dual-track framework for AI search strategy: one track focused on stable, enduring strategic insights that won't change every six weeks, and a second focused on rapid experimentation with new workflows and tools. He cautioned that AI-assisted content, while clearly the future, requires custom tooling for serious brands rather than generic AI writing tools.

Key Insights:

-AI search strategy requires simultaneous investment in two things: stable strategic foundations and a fast-moving experimentation workstream.

-Buying an off-the-shelf AEO monitoring tool produces data, but not a strategy — brands need custom thinking and custom tooling.

-AI-assisted content production, for enterprise brands, requires bespoke workflow integration, not commodity AI writers.

-The "insight" layer — understanding what's changing in your industry, for your brand, in your specific landscape — is the hardest and most valuable part.
