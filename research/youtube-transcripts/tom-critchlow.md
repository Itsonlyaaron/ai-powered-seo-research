Video 1 — What Your CMO Needs to Know About AI Search

Title: What Your CMO Needs to Know About AI Search w/ Tom Critchlow

Date: June 2026 (approx. 2 weeks before research date)

Platform: YouTube / Siege Media

URL: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=MTBIMRF1DLE

Short Summary: Ross Hudgens and Tom Critchlow explore what GEO (Generative Engine Optimization) actually means for CMOs in 2026 and why nobody has fully solved it yet. Tom argues that AI search measurement looks far more like brand measurement than classic SEO: less about prompt monitoring and keyword rankings, more about share of model, awareness, and how LLMs characterize a brand — not just whether they cite it.


Key Insights:

-Rank trackers were never measuring the full buying funnel — AI search makes this gap catastrophically obvious.

-The question to ask isn't "how do I rank for more prompts?" but "how does the model characterize my brand when someone is making a buying decision?"

-Brand, PR, and product teams — not SEO teams — tend to own the inputs that move GEO outcomes.

-The personalized, push-not-pull future of search fundamentally changes how marketers should build audience strategy.






Video 2 — The Future of Search & The Death of Links (BRXND NYC 2025)

Title: The Future of Search & The Death of Links | Tom Critchlow | BRXND NYC 2025

Date: October 16, 2025

Platform: YouTube / BRXND

URL: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=z1qxqB_Cg3Q

Short Summary: Tom's keynote at the BRXND NYC 2025 conference examining what SEO, GEO, and AIEO actually mean for consumer behavior. He traces how the search landscape is evolving in the age of AI and what it means for brand discovery when links — the foundational currency of the web — become secondary to AI-generated summaries.


Key Insights:

-The shift from "10 blue links" to AI-generated answers represents a fundamental change in how brands reach customers, not an incremental evolution.

-Consumer behavior at the top of the funnel is changing faster than brands are adapting their strategies.

-SEO, GEO, and AIEO are not the same discipline — marketers need to stop conflating them.

-Link-centric thinking will remain partially valid as long as AI systems rely on web retrieval, but the window for link-only strategies is closing.






Video 3 — AI SEO Deep Dive with Dan Petrovic (DEJAN AI)

Title: AI SEO Deep Dive — Tom Critchlow & Dan Petrovic [DEJAN AI]

Date: November 19, 2025

Platform: YouTube / DEJAN AI

URL: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-f9QCqTqdTA

Short Summary: A technical deep-dive going beyond LinkedIn hype to examine how LLMs like Gemini and GPT actually work under the hood for search visibility. Tom and Dan Petrovic explore a key industry shift: from Click-Through Rate (CTR) optimization to Selection Rate Optimization (SRO) — the idea that AI search requires a fundamentally different measurement paradigm than traditional SEO.


Key Insights:

-Static prompt tracking lists are the wrong measurement approach for AI visibility — you're measuring symptoms, not causes.

-The shift from CTR to SRO (Selection Rate Optimization) reframes what success in AI search looks like.

-No one — including Google and Anthropic — fully understands why LLMs surface specific brands; emergent properties mean even model makers lack complete interpretability.

-Model distillation techniques are emerging as a way to probe what AI systems actually "know" about brands and companies.
