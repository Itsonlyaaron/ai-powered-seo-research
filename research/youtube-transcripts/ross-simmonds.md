Video 1 — Content Distribution in the Age of AI
Title: Content Distribution in the Age of AI — Ross Simmonds, Foundation Marketing
Date: November 13, 2025 (recorded October 8, 2025 at Zero Click by Profound, New York City)
Platform: YouTube
URL: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VXxFJAg7YJw
Short Summary: Recorded live at Profound's Zero Click conference, Simmonds delivers a densely tactical keynote on how content distribution must be rethought in an era where AI systems — not search clicks — determine brand exposure. He argues that the old "publish and pray" model is dead, and that brands must architect a distribution engine that feeds LLMs, Google, and community platforms simultaneously. He connects the "Create Once, Distribute Forever" framework directly to GEO outcomes.

Key Insights:
Distribution is no longer a post-publish activity — it must be designed into content from the first draft, with each asset mapped to the channels where LLMs and buyers congregate.
The "Create Once, Distribute Forever" principle is more urgent now than ever: a single well-distributed piece can train AI models, surface in Reddit threads, and earn YouTube citations simultaneously.
GEO monitoring tools (e.g., Profound) allow teams to track which specific Reddit threads and stories are actively influencing LLM citations in real time — making distribution measurable.
Zero-click environments don't mean zero impact; brands cited frequently in AI answers build recognition even without direct traffic, which eventually converts at higher intent.



research II
Video 2 — How to Rank #1 When AI Is Taking Over ft. Ross Simmonds
Title: How to Rank #1 When AI Is Taking Over ft. Ross Simmonds
Date: December 3, 2024
Platform: YouTube (Marketing Against the Grain / HubSpot)
URL: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xLWBDKX-_3Q
Short Summary: Simmonds joins Kipp Bodnar and Kieran Flanagan to debate whether Google is dying as a traffic source and what marketers should do about it. He walks through the framework for winning visibility in an AI-first search environment, including why multi-channel distribution is the new SEO moat and how brands can build content that earns placement in AI-generated answers alongside traditional search rankings.

Key Insights:
Google is not dead, but the game has changed: traffic is concentrating in zero-click SERP features and AI overviews, shifting the value of content from clicks to citations.
Brands should build custom AI tools (e.g., GPTs) that solve audience-specific problems — these serve as distribution channels, lead magnets, and authority signals simultaneously.
The brands winning in 2025–2026 combine human emotional storytelling with AI-powered distribution at scale; neither alone is sufficient.
Experimentation is a structural requirement: 80% of effort should be proven tactics, 20% reserved for bold, channel-boundary-pushing experiments that competitors won't attempt.

Video 3 — B2B Content Marketing & Distribution — Drive 2024 (Exit Five)
Title: B2B Content Marketing & Distribution with Ross Simmonds, Founder and CEO of Foundation Marketing
Date: December 9, 2024
Platform: YouTube (Exit Five)
URL: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7_bFP2iVVN0
Short Summary: Recorded live at Exit Five's Drive 2024 event in Burlington, Vermont, Simmonds presents a playbook for B2B content that drives measurable revenue — not just traffic. He demonstrates with Foundation's own research how Reddit's organic visits surged from 200M to 500M+ annually by 2024, and why B2B brands cannot afford to treat it as a niche channel. He closes with a tactical breakdown of LinkedIn, Reddit, and community-based distribution.

Key Insights:
Newsletter open rates of 40% mean 60% of hard-earned subscribers never see a given send — republishing and multi-channel distribution is not optional, it's essential for reach.
Reddit's 500M+ monthly organic visits are driven by its licensing agreement with Google — making it both a search and AI training powerhouse that most B2B brands ignore.
"Replaying" your best-performing content on a regular cadence (monthly reshares, reposts) multiplies its impact with zero additional creation cost.
SERP domination — owning multiple positions for a query across different domains (your blog, Reddit, YouTube, G2) — is more powerful than ranking #1 alone.
